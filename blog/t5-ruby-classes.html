    Classes in Ruby

    I don't know if Ruby is truly "The Classy-ist of Them All" as I'm not familiar with other languages yet so don't quote me on that or anything.. Anyways, let's get down to business. What are classes?

    When you hear the word "class" what do you think of? Probably a room full of desks filled with students and a teacher right? Each class is filled with students of similar ages who listen to the teacher, take notes, learn, etc. In the Ruby world, the class that groups these students together would be a Ruby class, the students are the objects (or instances) of the Ruby class 'class', and the actions of note-taking, learning, etc would be the methods that each of the students (objects/instances) would be able to perform.

    Confused? Let's back up a bit. Classes in Ruby are just ways to group like methods together. Maybe you've got a method that adds two numbers. Maybe you've got another method that subtracts two numbers and yet another two that multiples and divides them. All these methods are related in that they deal with calculations. As such, you could store all these methods together in a class called Calculator.
